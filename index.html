2021 → 2023

Product Manager at Cogo

The climate crisis is a systemic problem which requires systemic change. But what does it mean to create systemic change?

Should Consumers change their purchasing choices? Or is it businesses that should change what they do? Or should the government be writing laws to require change? Or is it voters who need to act, to give their government a mandate for change?

Maybe it's everything, everywhere, all at once?

At Cogo I led development of a new product to help small businesses measure, understand and reduce their carbon footprint.

2015 → 2018

Software Engineer at Flick Electric

I worked at Flick Electric as a software engineer and team lead. Our innovation was in allowing consumers to purchase electricity at the highly variable spot market rate. We believed in disintermediation. In the power of raw capitalism to drive down power prices and make consumers respond to the environmental impact of their electricity use. It worked for a little while.

I developed carbon calculations and invented farting goats as a measure of carbon emissions. I later learned that goats are ruminants so most of their emissions come from burping.

I didn't love languages though, and also we did not make much money.
